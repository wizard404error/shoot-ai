📚 Tactical Knowledge Reference

Formations define the starting positions and shape of a team. Modern football uses formations as a base structure that shifts between defending and attacking phases.

4-4-2
GK · LB · CB · CB · RB · LM · CM · CM · RM · ST · ST

Classic balanced system. 4 defenders, 4 midfielders, 2 strikers.

  • Balanced defense/attack
  • Direct football, wide play
  • Midfield can be outnumbered (weakness)
4-3-3
GK · LB · CB · CB · RB · CM · CDM · CM · LW · ST · RW

Modern attacking system. Used by Barcelona, Liverpool.

  • High pressing, wing dominance
  • Ball possession focused
  • 3 forwards press high
4-2-3-1
GK · LB · CB · CB · RB · CDM · CDM · LW · CAM · RW · ST

Most balanced modern system.

  • 2 defensive midfield shields
  • 3 attacking midfielders
  • Control + defense stability
3-5-2
GK · CB · CB · CB · LWB · CM · CDM · CM · RWB · ST · ST

Wing-back system. Strong midfield control.

  • 3 center backs
  • Wing-backs provide width
  • 5 midfielders total
3-4-3
GK · CB · CB · CB · LM · CM · CM · RM · LW · ST · RW

Aggressive pressing system. High pressure, fast transitions.

  • 3 forwards press high
  • 4 midfielders control center
  • Requires fit wing-backs
4-1-2-1-2 Diamond
GK · LB · CB · CB · RB · CDM · LM · RM · CAM · ST · ST

Midfield diamond. Great for central control.

  • 4 defenders
  • Diamond midfield (1 DM, 2 wide, 1 AM)
  • 2 strikers up front

Passing Triangles are the foundation of possession football. Every great team builds passing triangles so the player on the ball always has 2 passing options.

🔺 Basic Triangle
        A
       / \
      B---C
  A = ball carrier
  B & C = passing options
  • Ball carrier always has 2 options
  • Prevents pressing traps
  • Creates quick combinations
  • Keeps possession stable
📐 Third Man Combination
  A → B (pass)
  B → A (return)
  A → C (switch)
  A-B-C involved

A one-two followed by a switch to a third player. Breaks defensive lines.

🎯 Where Triangles Appear
  • Midfield — most important zone
  • Wing play — fullback + winger + midfielder
  • Defense build-up — CB + CM + GK
  • Attacking third — striker + 2 supporting attackers

Pressing Systems define how and where a team applies pressure to win back possession.

🔥 High Press

Pressure opponent in their own half. Force mistakes near their goal.

  • Defensive line at ~55-60% pitch length
  • Forwards press opposition CBs
  • High risk, high reward
  • Used by: Liverpool (Klopp), Manchester City
⚖️ Mid Block

Pressure in the middle third. Balanced approach.

  • Defensive line at ~35-50% pitch length
  • Team compresses middle third
  • Waits for opponent to enter midfield
🛡 Low Block

Team defends deep in their own half. Compact and hard to break down.

  • Defensive line at ~30% pitch length
  • Two compact banks of 4
  • Invites opponent forward
  • Effective for counter-attacks
🎯 Trigger Pressing

Press only when specific cues occur:

  • Back pass to goalkeeper
  • Poor touch / bad control
  • Slow / sideways pass
  • Player receives with back to goal
  • Ball in pressing trap zone
⚡ Gegenpressing

Counter-pressing: Immediately press after losing the ball.

  • Win ball back in 5-8 seconds
  • Most dangerous time to concede
  • Used by: Klopp, Rangnick
🧱 Man vs Zonal
  • Man marking: Each player marks one opponent
  • Zonal marking: Players defend zones, not individuals
  • Hybrid marking: Mix of both (modern elite system)

Transitions are the 4 phases of football: Attack → Defensive Transition → Defense → Attacking Transition. Elite teams excel at transitions.

🔄 The 4 Phases
  Attack ←→ Defensive Transition
    ↑              ↑
    ↓              ↓
  Attacking ←   Defense
  Transition
⚡ Counter-Attack
  • Deep defensive block
  • Quick long passes after winning ball
  • Exploits opponent's empty space
  • Most effective within 5 seconds of regain
🛑 Defensive Transition
  • Immediate reaction after losing ball
  • First 5 seconds are critical
  • Counter-pressing or drop into shape
  • Communication between lines

Attacking Shapes describe the formation a team takes when in possession. Modern teams often use different shapes in attack vs defense.

🧩 3-2-5 in Attack

Elite attacking shape. Used by Manchester City, Arsenal, Barcelona.

  • 3 defenders stay back (2 CB + 1 DM drop)
  • 2 midfield anchors control center
  • 5 attackers push forward
  • Overloads opponent defense
  • Maximizes passing triangles
🔷 Diamond Midfield

4-1-2-1-2 shapes into a diamond in midfield:

  • 1 CDM at base
  • 2 wide midfielders
  • 1 CAM at tip
  • Great for central control
📏 Line Spacing
  • Compact: Lines close together (< 30m) — harder to break through
  • Stretched: Lines far apart (> 40m) — more space for attackers
  • Optimal: 30-40m between lines

Defensive Systems determine how a team organizes without the ball.

🧱 Low Block Defense
  • Team defends in own half
  • Two compact banks of 4
  • Hard to break down
  • Vulnerable to crosses
🔄 Rotations

Players swap positions dynamically in possession:

  • Fullback becomes midfielder
  • Midfielder becomes winger
  • Winger drops into midfield
  • Creates confusion for defenders
🚀 Overlapping Runs
  • Fullback runs past winger
  • Creates 2v1 on the flank
  • Opens crossing chances
